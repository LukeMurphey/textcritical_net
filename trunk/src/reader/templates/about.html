About TextCritical.com

Want to know more about the project?

Why make the site?

When I began to learn Koine Greek, I was surprised how difficult it was to find freely accessible ancient Greek works in the original languages online. It was particularly difficult to find morphologically parsed works other than the New Testament, the Septuagint and the Works of Homer. These works are available but often rather expensive. For example, the works of Flavius Josephus are available from Logos, but they are nearly $200! The most complete source of ancient Greek seemed to be Perseus.tufts.edu but the site is terribly slow at times, the design is dated and it doesn't work particularly well on mobile devices. Therefore, it seemed like someone needed to make a website

Alright, what are you doing to solve the problem?

The main goal is provide open access to ancient Greek works along with analytic tools that are useful for reading and analyzing these works (like morphological tagging). TextCritical.com is being designed to:
  1. Provide a modern user interface that works on mobile devices (i.e. "responsive")
  2. Provide useful study tools (like morphological tagging)
